Rhetoric

Rhetoric is the art of persuading or making an argument. Due to some source of exigence, authors use:

  • Words, marked by diction: tropes

  • Sentences, marked by syntax: schemes

  • Paragraphs, marked by structure: forms, genres, plots, dispositio

To depict:

  • Ideas: theses, themes

  • Imagery: settings, characters, actions

  • Emotion: tones, moods

Appealing to:

  • Ethos, or the author: credentials, anecdotes

  • Logos, or the subject: induction, deduction, logic

  • Pathos, or the audience: sympathetic, antithetic, apathetic

to prove a point and fulfil their purpose.
